ELECTRON
e-
Electron
symbol: e-
mass: m=9.109×10-31 kg
charge: q=−1 e = −1.6×10-19C
An electron is a subatomic particle with a negative elementary electric charge. It is a fundamental constituent of matter and belongs to the lepton family of particles.
PROTON
p+
Proton
symbol: p+
mass: m=1.673×10-27 kg
charge: q=+1 e = +1.6×10-19C
A proton is a subatomic particle with a positive electric charge. Protons, along with neutrons, make up the nucleus of an atom. The number of protons in an atom determines which chemical element it is.
NEUTRON
n0
Neutron
symbol: n0
mass: m=1.675×10-27 kg
charge: q=0
A neutron is a subatomic particle with no electric charge. Neutrons are found in the nucleus of atoms alongside protons. They have slightly more mass than protons and help stabilize the nucleus.
UP QUARK
u
Up Quark
symbol: u
mass: m=2.2×10-30 kg
charge: q=+2/3 e
The up quark is a type of elementary particle and a fundamental constituent of matter. It is one of the six quarks in the Standard Model of particle physics. Up quarks combine with down quarks to form protons and neutrons.
DOWN QUARK
d
Down Quark
symbol: d
mass: m=4.7×10-30 kg
charge: q=-1/3 e
The down quark is a type of elementary particle and a fundamental constituent of matter. It is one of the six quarks in the Standard Model of particle physics. Down quarks combine with up quarks to form protons and neutrons.
